# Data_Analysis_Project
This project presents a comprehensive retail sales analysis using Microsoft Excel and PostgreSQL to uncover valuable business insights from transactional sales data.
Project Overview
This project analyzes 1,000 retail transactions to uncover customer purchasing patterns, sales performance, seasonal trends, and product category performance. Microsoft Excel was used for data cleaning, pivot table analysis, and dashboard creation.
The objective was to transform raw sales data into meaningful business insights that support strategic decision-making.
Questions
This analysis answers the following questions:
•	Which product category generates the highest revenue?
•	Which months have the strongest sales?
•	Does customer gender influence purchasing?
•	Which age group contributes the most revenue?
•	Which quarter performs best?
•	How many products are sold across each category?

Business Recommendations
•	Expand the Electronics product line since it generates the highest revenue.
•	Increase promotional campaigns for Beauty products to improve sales.
•	Introduce seasonal discounts during Q3 and September to address weaker sales.
•	Continue targeting adult customers through loyalty programs and personalized marketing.
•	Capitalize on strong Q4 demand by increasing inventory before the holiday season.
•	Since Clothing sells the highest volume, consider cross-selling accessories and complementary products to increase average order value.

Project Conclusion
This project demonstrates how Excel dashboards can transform raw transactional data into actionable business insights. By analyzing customer demographics, product performance, and seasonal sales trends, the business can make informed decisions on inventory planning, marketing strategies, and customer targeting. The analysis shows that Electronics are the highest revenue-generating category, Clothing leads in sales volume, adults are the dominant customer segment, and Q4 is the strongest sales period. These findings provide a data-driven foundation for improving business performance and future growth.

Electronics generated the highest revenue, although Clothing closely followed behind. Beauty products produced the lowest sales, suggesting opportunities for improved promotions or product expansion.
